# BioHarmony-AI-assisted-Research-on-Biodynamic-Agrotourism-and-Regenerative-Rural-Living
Developing an AI-assisted framework for regenerative agrotourism that combines biodynamic farming, ecosystem restoration, renewable energy, and holistic health. Research focuses on creating sustainable rural environments that enhance biodiversity and improve quality of life.

BioHarmony is an interdisciplinary research initiative exploring how artificial intelligence can support the design of biodynamic agrotourism ecosystems that improve human well-being while regenerating the natural environment.

The project combines knowledge from regenerative agriculture, biodynamic farming, environmental psychology, sustainable architecture, ecology, and human health. The objective is to develop evidence-informed guidelines for creating agrotourism environments that promote physical health, mental restoration, biodiversity, and low-impact living.

AI will be used to:

review and synthesize scientific literature on regenerative and biodynamic farming;
compare international case studies of successful eco-farms and rural tourism;
analyze environmental indicators and biodiversity practices;
assist in designing self-sufficient farm models integrating renewable energy, rainwater harvesting, food forests, and natural building materials;
develop visitor education programs focused on reconnecting people with nature;
generate documentation, reports, research summaries, and sustainability assessments.

The long-term vision is to create a practical framework for rural communities and small farms seeking to combine sustainable agriculture, ecological tourism, and holistic well-being while preserving local ecosystems and cultural heritage.

Research topics include:

regenerative agriculture
biodiversity restoration
carbon sequestration
soil microbiome
biodynamic cultivation
sustainable tourism
ecological architecture
renewable energy systems
circular economy
environmental education
nature-based mental health
ecosystem resilience

Expected outcomes include research reports, design guidelines, educational materials, and an open knowledge base supporting environmentally responsible agrotourism development.
